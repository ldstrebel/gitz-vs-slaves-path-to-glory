## Arc 2 — The Shattered Firmament

**Status:** Battle 7 upcoming (The Crystal Siege). Arc closes at Battle 9 or 10.

**Arc premise:** The Gitz have broken the Chaos Lord's warband and drained the Chaos Sorcerer to near-nothing. But the Slaves to Darkness haven't collapsed — they've *retreated into their stronghold*, the Crystalline Nexus, where the geometry of reality is literally on their side. The arc asks: can a shattered warband hold a fortified position long enough to rebuild its identity, or does the unstoppable fungal tide overwhelm them before they can become something new?

**Counter-trope intent:** The Slaves shouldn't go out with a whimper. A losing warband finding meaning *through* defeat is more interesting than one that just loses. The Gitz shouldn't feel like winners — Bossy is haunted, the warband is becoming something stranger than just "winning grots".

## Active Narrative Threads
*Updated after each battle via Arc Compass answers.*

### Grot Bossy / Gloomspite Gitz
- **The World-Eater's Voice:** Bossy hears fragmented, agonised whispers from the dying World-Eater. Post-B6, the Chaos Lord's death seems to have changed the *pitch* of what Bossy hears. Does he understand any of it? Does he want to?
- **God-Batch Residue:** Still on him. Hasn't been washed off. Is it doing something?
- **The Titan's Whisper (B6 rank-up):** Bossy killed the most dangerous man on the field. Some of the grots are starting to look at him differently — not as just the boss, but as something chosen.
- **Loonboss on Squig — Cracked Ribs:** Can't issue Commands. A proud unit reduced to following. How does that land?
- **Boingrot Bounderz — Adrenaline Junkies:** They're elite now. Starting to take *risks* no sane squig rider would take.
- **Loonsmasha Fanatics — Momentum Crash:** Their Elite path makes them devastating on contact. But post-crash they're dangerously exposed. This is a unit that lives and dies by its opening move.

### Gunnar Brand / Slaves to Darkness
- **Default Commander:** With the Chaos Lord dead and the Sorcerer drained, Gunnar Brand commands by default. He didn't ask for this. What does that mean for a man who survived by following orders?
- **The Numbness (B6 rank-up):** Ward 6+. Physically, something has shut off in him. Whether that's a gift or a warning sign is a story question.
- **Singri Brand — The Ghost-Daughter:** Has the Father's Keeper scar. Unexplored. Her being called "Ghost-Daughter" while alive is a thread that wants pulling.
- **Chaos Sorcerer Lord — Tethered & Drained:** Still alive. Still being siphoned. The warband's martyrdom theology says suffering *earns* something. How long before the Sorcerer becomes the symbol rather than the general?
- **Chaos Chosen — Fractured Plate:** Serious scar (-1 Save). The most elite unit in the StD warband is now vulnerable. They probably don't react to that well.
- **The Mutalith Vortex Beast — Chained:** Still with the StD. It destroyed the God-Batch vats by instinct. Now it's a liability chained to a broken warband. What does it want?

## Arc 2 Battle Themes — Ideas Only

### B7 — The Crystal Siege (upcoming)
**Location:** Crystalline Nexus / Spire of Reflections. StD stronghold. 4 Fractal Nodes.

**Theme:** *The fortress psychology.* The Slaves are defending something — the last place they have. The Gitz are attacking something they don't understand. Crystal reflects everything, including things you'd rather not see.

**Character development seeds:**
- Gunnar Brand's first battle as commander, by default, under siege conditions. Does he freeze, adapt, or overcorrect?
- Bossy assaulting a place that reflects his own image back at him — how does a grot who's hearing a dead god process that?
- The Sorcerer as *asset or problem* — is the tethered Sorcerer a strategic weapon or a liability the Chosen have to protect?

**Counter-tropes to explore:**
- Don't let the siege feel like a straightforward "attacker wins." The stronghold should do something to the Gitz.
- The Slaves should feel different defending — not cowardly but *purposeful*, like they've decided something in the retreat.
- If the Gitz win, it's not a clean victory. If the Slaves hold, it's not just "they defended well" — something changes.

**Clever twist ideas (pick one when building the battle):**
- The Crystalline Nexus reflects ambient magic — the Sorcerer's drain *powers* something in the walls
- Objectives might shift position as the crystal geometry "refolds" each round
- Bossy's World-Eater frequency matters here — the Nexus may resonate with something from the God-Batch residue

### B8 — Arc Mid-Point (post-siege consequences)
**Theme:** *What did the siege cost?* One side won — but nothing comes free.

**Character development seeds:**
- If StD won B7: The Sorcerer survives the drain but is *changed*. The martyrdom theology crystallises into something coherent and dangerous. Gunnar Brand stops being accidental commander.
- If Gitz won B7: The Nexus is breached but Bossy walked out of a crystal mirror and something followed him back. The Chosen are scattered but not dead. Revenge is a slow pressure.
- Singri Brand's scar should *come alive* here — whatever "The Father's Keeper" means, B8 is where we find out.

**Clever twist ideas:**
- A battle fought in the aftermath of destruction — not fresh terrain but the ruins of what was
- Stakes aren't objectives but *people* — escort/extraction scenario? Whose people?
- The Mutalith's behaviour changes post-siege. A Magic-Eater surrounded by shattered crystal full of drained arcane energy does... what?

### B9/B10 — Arc Closure: The Reckoning
**Theme:** *The arc answer.* What was The Shattered Firmament actually about?

**Design intent:**
- The arc should close on a character moment, not just a score. Whatever the battle result, both warbands leave changed.
- Bossy either takes a step toward the World-Eater and becomes something post-grot, or pulls back and stays grounded in his warband.
- The Slaves to Darkness either complete the martyrdom arc (Sorcerer becomes a symbol, warband becomes a cult) or fractures entirely (Gunnar Brand goes his own way, Singri Brand disappears).
- Hard narrative reset follows: new location, different stakes, same characters but with the weight of Arc 2 on them.

**Arc 3 seeds (plant in Arc Compass questions at B9/B10):**
- Where does the World-Eater's corpse eventually *land*? The Gitz have been fighting for debris sites — eventually they reach the source.
- What's the Chaos faction's relationship to Khorne given the World-Eater's death? Is there a replacement power? A competition?
- A new force entering the realmshard — someone else wants the debris too.
