## Arc 2 — The Shattered Firmament

**Status:** Battle 8 complete. Battle 9 upcoming. Arc closes at Battle 10.

**Arc premise:** The Gitz have broken the Chaos Lord's warband and drained the Chaos Sorcerer to near-nothing. But the Slaves to Darkness haven't collapsed — they've *retreated into their stronghold*, the Crystalline Nexus, where the geometry of reality is literally on their side. The arc asks: can a shattered warband hold a fortified position long enough to rebuild its identity, or does the unstoppable fungal tide overwhelm them before they can become something new?

**Counter-trope intent:** The Slaves shouldn't go out with a whimper. A losing warband finding meaning *through* defeat is more interesting than one that just loses. The Gitz shouldn't feel like winners — Bossy is haunted, the warband is becoming something stranger than just "winning grots".

### B9/B10 — Arc Closure: The Reckoning
**Theme:** *The arc answer.* What was The Shattered Firmament actually about?

**Design intent:**
- The arc should close on a character moment, not just a score. Whatever the battle result, both warbands leave changed.
- Bossy either takes a step toward the World-Eater and becomes something post-grot, or pulls back and stays grounded in his warband.
- The Slaves to Darkness either complete the martyrdom arc (Sorcerer becomes a symbol, warband becomes a cult) or fractures entirely (Gunnar Brand goes his own way, Singri Brand disappears).
- Hard narrative reset follows: new location, different stakes, same characters but with the weight of Arc 2 on them.

**Arc 3 seeds (plant in Arc Compass questions at B9/B10):**
- Where does the World-Eater's corpse eventually *land*? The Gitz have been fighting for debris sites — eventually they reach the source.
- What's the Chaos faction's relationship to Khorne given the World-Eater's death? Is there a replacement power? A competition?
- A new force entering the realmshard — someone else wants the debris too.

## How to Use This Document

**Campaign Engine (battle generation):**
→ Complete **Stage 4: The Architect** of the Orchestration Flow.
→ Read Active Threads before generating a battle.
→ Draw on Theme + Character seeds for hook and twist design.
→ Use the new Underdog Formula (Total Wins + 90% GP difference) to identify the Campaign Underdog.
→ Build the **Dual Twist Tables** (one 1D6 table per faction) to ensure the current underdog always has a thematic comeback mechanic.
→ Never copy these ideas directly — let them *inform* original ideas.

**Aftermath Gem (post-battle):**
→ Complete **Stage 1 (Survey) -> Stage 2 (Story) -> Stage 3 (Quartermaster)** of the Orchestration Flow.
→ Run the Arc Compass survey after each battle (Stage 1).
→ Record player responses in the Arc Compass Response Log.
→ Update Active Threads based on rank-ups, scars, and player choices.
→ Move "upcoming" battle themes forward; add a new stub for the next battle.
