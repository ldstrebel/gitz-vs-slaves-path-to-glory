# Aftermath of Sigmar: Campaign Engine

Welcome to the **"Aftermath of Sigmar"** Narrative Campaign Engine for Warhammer Age of Sigmar (4th Edition). 

This system gamifies the narrative space between your regular games. The core philosophy is "Narrative First, Rules Second"—the numbers fuel the story, not the other way around. 

This README serves as the complete end-to-end manual. Below is what you (the user) need to do during a campaign step, what the AI handles, and exactly which files get updated under the hood.

---

## 🎲 The End-to-End Campaign Loop

Here is your exact operating procedure for running a campaign iteration.

### Step 1: Play the Game
Go play your game of Warhammer Age of Sigmar using the battleplan generated for you! Roll dice, have fun, and track which units were destroyed, who scored the most points, and who did something heroic.

### Step 2: The Raw Data Dump (User)
Once the game ends, you need to collect the raw data.
1. Fill out a fresh copy of the **`aftermath-template.md`** with the raw mechanical results: 
   - Who won? What was the final VP? 
   - Which units survived, and which were destroyed?
   - Any quests completed?
2. If your Army Roster changed, export the new roster as raw text from the AoS App.

### Step 3: Trigger the Engine (User)
In this chat, simply say: **"Process aftermath for Battle [X]"** and paste your filled-out `aftermath-template.md` (and optional roster updates). 
> **Important:** The AI has strict "Zero-Math" data integrity rules. The AI will never modify your roster points.

### Step 4: The Narrative Survey (AI & User)
The AI will process the threshold math (calculating Glory Points, identifying who ranked up or gained a scar) and then **HALT**. 
The AI will generate an **Aftermath Survey** for you to answer. You must tell the AI:
- *Why* did your General level up? Do you want Toughness or Speed?
- *How* did the Chaos Chosen get their "Severe" Scar? What is the injury?
- *What* are your character's motivations going forward?

### Step 5: The Master Orchestration (AI)
Once you reply to the survey, the AI automatically executes a 3-stage mechanical update sequence. **You just sit back and watch.**
1. **The Chronicler:** The AI writes a rich narrative chapter summarizing the battle based on your survey answers.
2. **The Quartermaster:** The AI updates the mechanical databases (GP, Win/Loss, unit stats, scars).
3. **The Architect:** The AI checks the Underdog formulas, generates Dual Twist Tables, and automatically creates the rules, manifesto, and tactical map for your next game.

## 📁 What Files Are Updated?

This framework separates the **Story** (narrative lore) from the **State** (mechanical rules). Here is the breakdown of exactly which files are touched and by whom.

### User Edited Files:
*   **`aftermath-template.md`**: You fill this out with raw battle data to kick off the engine.

### AI Orchestrated Files:
During **Stage 2 (The Chronicler)**, the AI updates:
*   `Campaign Lore/campaign-novel.md`: The ongoing prose story of the campaign.
*   `Campaign Lore/Previous Battles.md`: A historical archive of who fought, who won, and what Twists were used.

During **Stage 3 (The Quartermaster)**, the AI updates the true mechanical state:
*   `Campaign Bible/Battle Bible.md`: The absolute source of truth for the campaign economy (Treasury, Wounds, Win/Loss Record, thresholds).
*   `Campaign Bible/character-development.md`: The hardcoded log of Path Abilities, Scars, and their in-game effects.
*   `AI Planning/arc-planning.md`: The AI's internal tracking of narrative plot threads.

During **Stage 4 (The Architect)**, the AI generates the future state:
*   `Campaign Lore/[Battle X]/Battle [X].md`: The complete ruleset, deployment, and objective map for your next battle.
*   `Campaign Lore/[Battle X]/battle_map.png`: Autogenerated tactical map for the upcoming confrontation.
