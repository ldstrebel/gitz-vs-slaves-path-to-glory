**Recap:**

> In the choked atmosphere of the Valley of Ash, the race for the World-Eater’s residue began with a frantic, clamoring charge from the Gloomspite Gitz. Seizing the initiative, Grot Bossy unleashed his squabbling horde upon the impact site, his Troggoths drooling at the scent of the pulsating, cosmic wound in the earth. The grots scrambled over the grey dunes, driven by a gluttonous madness to consume the "Star-Feast" before their rivals could draw breath. However, the Slaves to Darkness, though initially caught on the back foot and forced to fight from an underdog position, met the green tide with a wall of cold iron and stoic discipline.
> The turning point came as the Chaos Sorcerer Lord began to weave the raw, volatile magic of the crash site into a weapon, turning the very prize of the battle against the looters. The Gloomspite assault shattered against the Chaos battleline; for every inch of ground the grots gained, they paid in heaps of fungal flesh. The chaotic energy of the Bad Moon was eclipsed by the regimented cruelty of the Dark Gods, and what was meant to be a feast for the Gitz quickly devolved into a massacre. The Chaos warriors systematically dismantled the Mob, denying them the celestial power they so desperately craved.
> In the end, the Gitz were thoroughly defeated, reduced to scrambling in the dirt and reaching desperately for just one more glowing shroom before being routed from the field. The Slaves to Darkness claimed the site, securing a minor but decisive victory as they siphoned the energy and prepared to march on. But amidst the retreat, a new, venomous resolve took root in Grot Bossy’s heart. This was no longer just a race for power; it was personal. As the Chaos warband turned toward their homelands, the shadows behind them began to move—the Gitz would trail them into the very jaws of hell to steal back what was theirs.
