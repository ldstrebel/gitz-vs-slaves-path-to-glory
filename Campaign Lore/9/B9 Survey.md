## 🧭 The Arc Compass

**Question 1: The "Snarlboss Infire." A Snarlboss was killed by a Friendly Fire accident in the opening minutes. How does Grot Bossy spin this to the mob?**

- [ ] **Sacrifice to the Signal:** Bossy claims the World-Eater "needed a snack" to clear the air for his next cast.
- [x] **The Chosen Scapegoat:** Bossy blames the snarlboss for "trying to steal the frequency" and orders the shooters to be promoted.
- [ ] **A Grot's Intuition:** The mob believes the dying snarlboss saw "The Truth" and had to be silenced by the Bad Moon itself.

**Question 2: Anvil "cut his own hand" to bind a spore cluster, acting with a zealotry that borders on biological desperation. What is happening to the machine?**

- [ ] **The Awakening of the Flesh:** The World-Eater's energy is forcing life back into the rusted iron.
- [ ] **Desperate Integration:** He is trying to "taste" the power to understand why the Gitz are so obsessed with it.
- [ ] **The Core's Command:** He was not cutting himself; the Shard was pulling the blood *out* of him to complete the binding.

**Question 3: The Slaves to Darkness secured a Major Victory but lost their entire leadership core (Lord, Sorcerer, Chosen). As they retreat to the Crystalline Nexus, what is the mood of the survivors?**

- [ ] **Absolute Zealotry:** The cost was high, but the " Stillness" is worth any price.
- [ ] **Fractured Resolve:** Without the Lord's paranoia or the Sorcerer's vision, the Warriors and the Beast are merely wandering ghosts.
- [ ] **The Power of the Martyr:** The blood of the fallen heroes has sanctified the Nexus; they are stronger in death than they ever were in life.
