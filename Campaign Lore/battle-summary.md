## Battle 1 — Feast of the Star-Fall

### Narrative Hook
The World-Eater's first debris field has just crashed to earth — craters still smoking, celestial matter pulsing with raw power. Neither army has a plan beyond *get there first*. The Gitz swarm out of tunnels lured by the Bad Moon glow; the Chaos host marches under direct orders to claim every shard as tribute. This is the opening shot of the Scavenger's Race. No speeches, no banners — just two armies sprinting toward the same crater.

### Narrative Result
The Slaves to Darkness dominated from the outset. The Chaos Lord cut down Grot Bossy while the Chaos Chosen tore apart the Fanatics and Loonsmasha. The Mutalith Vortex Beast drove off the Dankhold Troggoth. The centerpiece secured was the **"Smoking Heart"** — a debris cluster pulsing with furious, almost sentient rage energy, claimed by the Chaos Sorcerer Lord. Of the entire Gitz warband, only the Loonboss on Giant Cave Squig survived, fleeing into the dark. The Gitz lost their general on Day 1. The Chaos force first tasted what it means to hold the World-Eater's power.

**Unresolved narrative threads:** What happened to the Smoking Heart? What did the Sorcerer Lord do with it? Why does the Mutalith seem personally drawn to cosmic debris?

### Battle Type & Design
- **Format:** Open engagement, 750 pts
- **Board:** Standard open field (no documented layout — fought early, described retroactively)
- **Objectives:** Standard crash-site control objectives (symmetrical). No secondary mechanics documented.
- **Objective interaction:** Passive — hold more objectives at end of turns. No special actions, no asymmetry, no terrain interaction rules.
- **Dominant terrain feel:** Smoking craters, open sightlines, no chokepoints.

## Battle 2 — The Trail of Glowing Spores

### Narrative Hook
The Gitz are licking their wounds but Grot Bossy refuses to quit — the "Weeping Lung" impact site is oozing with mutative celestial spore-matter, and the Bad Moon's pull is undeniable. He's practically dragging his survivors by their ears. The Chaos force, bloated with confidence from their first victory, march to claim another site — only this time the Gitz are ready to be angry about it.

### Narrative Result
A complete reversal. The Gitz tore through the Chaos line — Grot Bossy personally killed the Chaos Sorcerer Lord in revenge for Battle 1. The Chaos Legionnaires were shredded by the Sporesplatta Fanatics. The Mutalith fell to a Loonboss. Only the Chaos Chosen — elite veterans — walked out alive. Meanwhile, the Dankhold Troggoth gorged on so much raw celestial debris that it began to *visibly mutate mid-battle*. Grot Bossy secured the **Star-Mold Harvest** — a satchel of glowing spores that refilled his Potion Brew, already humming with strange new properties.

**Unresolved narrative threads:** What is the Dankhold Troggoth becoming? How much does the Star-Mold transformation affect the Sporesplatta Fanatics who died empowered by it? Why did the Chaos Chosen survive when everyone else didn't — are they truly the elite, or just lucky?

### Battle Type & Design
- **Format:** Open engagement, 800 pts
- **Board:** Standard open field with impact crater terrain (no documented layout — described retroactively)
- **Objectives:** Standard crash-site control objectives (symmetrical). The Gitz also completed an active **Quest objective** mid-battle (Star-Mold Harvest).
- **Objective interaction:** Passive hold objectives plus a concurrent quest tracker running independently. No special actions or terrain abilities.
- **Dominant terrain feel:** Wet, spore-choked ground, visceral biological texture from the "Weeping Lung" name.

## Battle 3 — The Spinal Ridge

### Narrative Hook
A massive ridge of calcified World-Eater spine has erupted from the earth, forming a wall across the battlefield. Glowing fungus clings to the ancient bone; volatile energy hums from within. The Gitz don't want to hold the spine — they want to *crack it open and eat what's inside*. The Chaos force wants to plant altars on it, consecrate the site in blood, and bind its power to the Ruinous Gods. Fundamentally incompatible goals that guarantee neither side can win on the other's terms.

### Narrative Result
A grinding Gitz minor victory. Grot Bossy fell again — proving he can kill a Sorcerer Lord but can't stay alive himself. The Dankhold Troggoth — now visibly mutated and increasingly feral — killed the Mutalith Vortex Beast in brutal close quarters. The freshly-recruited Squig Herd exploded into the Chaos side and ripped apart Gunnar Brand, Singri Brand, and The Oathsworn Kin — three newly introduced StD warriors who were immediately killed by grass-roots Squig enthusiasm. The Chaos force was further gutted. The Gitz chipped enough marrow loose to fuel their next push. **The site was not consecrated. The altars remain unfinished.**

**Unresolved narrative threads:** What were Gunnar Brand, Singri Brand, and the Oathsworn Kin actually *trying* to do — were they specialists sent for a specific ritual? The unfinished Chaos altar on the Spinal Ridge — does it leave a mark on the landscape? The Dankhold Troggoth is now something else: what exactly?

### Battle Type & Design
- **Format:** Custom asymmetrical battleplan, ~800 pts. 44" × 60", long-edge deployment.
- **Board layout:**
  - **North (StD):** Ruined Outpost (Cover)
  - **No Man's Land North (12"–22"):** Open ground + Acid Pool on left flank (Hazardous — end move here, roll D6, on 1 = D3 mortal damage)
  - **Center Killzone (22"–38"):** Three Spine terrain pieces forming a Obscuring wall across the board with 3 objectives nested on/in them. Impassable at base unless unit has Fly/Wall-crawler.
  - **No Man's Land South (38"–48"):** Open ground + Fungal Forest on right flank (Cover: +1 Save vs shooting)
  - **South (Gitz):** Cave Entrance (Cover)
- **Objectives:** 3 spine objectives. Main center spine (**Prime Vertebra**) worth **2VP** instead of 1VP. Scored at end of each turn.
- **Asymmetric terrain bonuses:** Moonclan units treat spine as Inspiring (+1 Control); StD Hero units treat spine as Arcane (attempt 1 unbind within 3")
- **Objective interaction — asymmetric secondaries:**
  - **Gitz:** *"Crack the Bone"* — Hero phase action, unit within 3" of spine terrain, roll D6: 3+ = 1VP instantly; 1 = D3 mortal damage. Max 1 attempt per terrain piece per turn. Gitz are rewarded for actively using the terrain as a tool, trading safety for points.
  - **StD:** *"Consecrate in Blood"* — passive aura, score 1VP whenever an enemy is destroyed within 6" of any spine terrain. Rewards the Chaos player for forcing engagements into the center killzone rather than flanking around it.
- **Design tension:** The Spine wall forces armies through or around it. Gitz want to stop and mine; Chaos want enemies dead right there. The wall creates congestion that benefits the Consecrate mechanic while the Mining mechanic rewards Gitz for holding still despite that danger.
- **Underdog table (StD):** Calcified Trap (halve enemy Move), Pulse of Rage (+1 Charge), Marrow Geyser (Heal 1 wound near an objective)

## Battle 4 — The Slaughter at the Crater's Edge

### Narrative Hook
Following the Spinal Ridge victory, the Gloomspite Gitz cornered the Slaves to Darkness as they attempted to haul away a massive, volatile chunk of the World-Eater's cosmic residue — consolidating their power before the Gitz could regroup. Grot Bossy ordered a reckless, all-out assault. This wasn't a plan; it was a controlled riot. The Gitz won by throwing enough fungal bodies at the iron line until the iron bent.

### Narrative Result
A brutal and decisive Gitz objective victory, **15 to 4**, despite the Chaos force racking up an extraordinary body count. The battle was defined by hero-on-hero carnage on both sides:

- **The Fall of the Beast:** The plain Loonboss — nothing special, just furious — landed the killing blow on the Mutalith Vortex Beast, toppling the abomination in a moment of drug-fueled, inexplicable heroism.
- **The Chaos Lord's Rampage:** Enraged at the Mutalith's fall, the Chaos Lord personally executed the Loonboss who killed it, then waded through the chaos to cut down Grot Bossy himself.
- **Death from Above:** With the Chaos Lord distracted on his killing spree, the Boingrot Bounderz launched a devastating flanking bounce, flying straight over the front lines to assassinate both the Chaos Sorcerer Lord and Singri Brand.
- **The Unbreakable Chosen:** The Chaos Chosen lived up to their name — forming an unbreakable wall, they systematically destroyed the Loonboss on Giant Cave Squig, a Rockgut Troggoth, and a full unit of Loonsmasha Fanatics.
- **The Fanatics' Toll:** The dying Loonsmasha Fanatics, before being butchered by the Chosen, caved in the chest of Gunnar Brand. He survived. Barely.

Despite the Chaos Lord and Chosen fighting like demigods, the Slaves lost their Sorcerer Lord, the Mutalith, and the ground. The Gitz won the objectives comprehensively. **This 15-4 defeat is what pushed the Slaves to Darkness into the desperate underdog position entering Battle 5** — no caster, no monster, no momentum.

**Unresolved narrative threads:** Grot Bossy was killed by the Chaos Lord but later revived/healed — what does his body remember of that death? The Chaos Lord and the Chosen survived and are now the last credible fighting core of the StD warband; how does that shape their identity going forward? Gunnar Brand survived a chest wound from a Fanatics ball — what kind of scar does that leave, and does it affect how he fights?

### Battle Type & Design
- **Format:** Standard objective engagement, ~800 pts. Open-field with timed scoring.
- **Board:** Outdoor field adjacent to the World-Eater debris crash site. The Chaos force was mid-extraction — hauling a massive shard of cosmic matter — when the Gitz hit. Layout was relatively open compared to later interior battles.
- **Objectives:** Standard hold-and-score objectives. Final score **15–4** strongly implies timed multi-objective scoring over several rounds, not asymmetric conditions.
- **Objective interaction:** Passive control scoring. The Gitz ignored the Chaos killing machine and played the objectives; the Slaves to Darkness focused on eliminating Gitz leaders and largely ignored board control — the structural reason for the lopsided score despite the body count going in StD's favour.
- **Design note:** No special environmental rules documented. The most notable "mechanic" was purely human — the Gitz player refusing to engage the Chaos Lord's death-machine and instead flooding objectives while trading attrition deliberately.

## Battle 5 — The Synapse-Storm

### Narrative Hook
The Hollow Heart is conquered. The Gitz have dragged crude brewing vats into the **Titan's Brain-Pan** — the hollow skull-chamber still crackling with residual psychic energy. They are siphoning cerebral fluid to brew the **"God-Batch"** — a draught capable of rewriting Grot Bossy's biology from grot to divine avatar. The vats are bubbling. The air smells of ozone and madness. The Chaos force blows a breach in the skull wall. Through the smoke and debris they see the vats, see Bossy leaning towards one with a ladle. They have **one objective**: smash the equipment before he takes the first sip. This is the closest the campaign has come to a true assassination run.

### Narrative Result
At the last possible second, the Mutalith Vortex Beast — drunk on residual God-Batch vapour and acting entirely on instinct — rampaged through the vat chamber and obliterated all three brewing vessels. Grot Bossy was drenched in sticky cerebral God-Batch residue but never got his sip. He is not a god. He *heard* something, though — fragmented, agonised whispers from the World-Eater's dying mind now leaking through the spill. Meanwhile, the Chaos Chosen and the Rockgut Troggoths annihilated each other in a grinding mutual destruction that left both units devastated. **The Sorcerer Lord died in this battle**, martyred by Fanatics while casting. The StD narrative codified around this: sacrifice and suffering as the price of victory.

**Unresolved narrative threads:** What exactly is Grot Bossy *hearing* from the World-Eater — fragments of memory, of rage, of hunger? The God-Batch residue is still on him — does it have any ongoing properties? The Mutalith acting on instinct to destroy the vats: was that coincidence or is it a *Magic-Eater* by nature (its chosen path), actively consuming ambient power to prevent others from having it? The Sorcerer Lord's martyrdom — the warband now orbits around sacrifice as a concept. Does this attract specific Chaos gods' attention?

### Battle Type & Design
- **Format:** Attack (StD) vs Defend (Gitz) multi-objective destruction. 4-round cap. 44" × 60".
- **Board layout:**
  - **North (StD):** Zones A, B, C — *Cranial Breach*, deployment within 15" of north edge. Zone B also has Skull Fragments (Impassable, blocks LOS) on borders B/E and C/F.
  - **Mid:** Zone D (west) = Vat Objective 1; Zone E (center) = **Exposed Nerve Cluster** (deadly hazard — end any move touching it = D3 mortal damage on 2+); Zone F (east) = Vat Objective 2
  - **South (Gitz):** Zones G, H, I — *Cerebral Ridge*, deployment within 15" of south edge. Zone H (center-south) = **God-Vat Objective 3** (the primary vat, deep in Gitz territory)
- **Objectives:** Three destructible vat-terrain pieces.
  - Vats: 12 HP, Save 4+. Fight back: end of combat phase, roll for each enemy unit within 1" — on 4+ = D3 mortal damage to that unit.
  - **Attacker (StD):** 5VP immediately per vat destroyed
  - **Defender (Gitz):** 3VP at end of each battle round per surviving vat
- **Objective interaction — secondaries:**
  - *Sever the Stem (StD):* 3VP if Grot Bossy is slain
  - *The Taste Test (Gitz):* 3VP if Bossy ends the battle within 3" of an active vat (he drinks)
  - *Synaptic Overload (Both):* 1VP each time an enemy unit is destroyed by the Synaptic Backlash environmental rule
- **Design tension:** The three vats are spread across the board (two mid-field, one deep in Gitz deployment). Destroying the two mid-field vats is achievable but leaves 10VP on the table from the God-Vat. The Exposed Nerve Cluster in the center acts as a natural deterrent midfield barrier — punishes rushing through. The Skull Fragments in the north block direct sightlines, forcing StD to route around. The vats fighting back punish melee-camping on them.
- **Environmental hazard:**
  - *Synaptic Backlash:* Start of every combat phase, **every single unit on the board** rolls a D6. On 1 = D3 mortal damage. Units with FLY trigger on 5+ instead (they rise into the arcing nerve discharge between ridges). Creates constant attrition pressure even during "inactive" turns.

## Battle 6 — The Ambush on the Shimmering Veldt

### Narrative Hook
The Slaves to Darkness thought they had broken contact. Bleeding and burdened by the chained Mutalith Vortex Beast, they were making a grueling march toward the safety of the Crystalline Nexus. But the Bad Moon's light reflects strangely off the Veldt's glassy surface — the Gitz hadn't lost the trail; they were just waiting for the Chaos vanguard to stretch too thin. Without warning, the horizon erupted in squig-shrieks and manic laughter. It wasn't a pitched battle — it was a high-speed predatory ambush designed to cripple the stragglers.

### Narrative Result
A 3-round surgical slaughter. Final score: **Gitz 8 — Slaves 2**. The Slaves to Darkness were forced into full retreat, abandoning ground and supplies.

- **The Snarlboss's Short-Lived Glory:** The newly mustered Snarlboss on War Chariot drove directly at the Chaos Chosen. Fatal miscalculation — the Chosen dismantled him and the chariot in seconds.
- **The Spin-Cycle of Vengeance:** The Loonsmasha Fanatics immediately hit those same Chosen with such force that breastplates caved — giving the Chosen their **Fractured Plate** scar and the Fanatics their **Momentum Crash** rank-up.
- **The Squig Rider's Blunder:** The Loonboss on Giant Cave Squig bounced too far and landed inside a Chaos Warrior shield wall. Survived. Barely. Left with **Cracked Ribs** and a new survival instinct: **Shiny Hat Trick**.
- **Rockgut Retribution:** The Rockgut Troggoth saw the Loonboss fall and walked through the Chaos Warriors until there were fewer of them.
- **The Duel of Leaders:** In the chaotic center, Grot Bossy — channeling the wild rage of the World-Eater — found a gap in the Chaos Lord's armour. **The Chaos Lord is dead.** Bossy gained *The Titan's Whisper*.

**Unresolved narrative threads:** With the Chaos Lord dead, who leads the Slaves to Darkness? Gunnar Brand by default? The Sorcerer is tethered and drained — the Chosen are orphaned of command. The Snarlboss died on his first battle without accomplishing anything meaningful. What does that do to warband morale? Bossy killed the most dangerous man in the enemy warband — does the World-Eater's frequency change in pitch after this?

**B6 Rank-ups (Gitz):** Grot Bossy — *Titan's Whisper* (Aspiring). Moonclan Shootas — *Coward's Cunning* (Aspiring). Boingrot Bounderz — *Adrenaline Junkies* (Elite). Loonsmasha Fanatics — *Momentum Crash* (Elite). Loonboss on Squig — *Shiny Hat Trick* (Aspiring).

**B6 Rank-ups (StD):** Gunnar Brand — *The Numbness* (Ward 6+). Singri Brand — *The Ghost-Daughter* (-1 Hit on Shooting).

**B6 Scars:** Gitz: Loonboss on Squig — *Cracked Ribs* (Serious, cannot issue Commands). StD: Chaos Chosen — *Fractured Plate* (Serious, -1 Save).

### Battle Type & Design
- **Format:** Skirmish ambush, 3-round cap. High-speed objective hold, asymmetric situational setup.
- **Board:** Open glass plains of the Shimmering Veldt. The Chaos force was mid-march with no prepared positions; the Gitz attacked from pre-set ambush points. Refracted light from the glass terrain made sightlines unreliable — Moonclan Shootas scored objectives by exploiting confusion rather than fighting.
- **Objectives:** Standard hold-and-score. The speed of the engagement and the Gitz's positional advantage meant the Slaves never achieved board control.
- **Objective interaction:** Passive. The defining "mechanic" was situational asymmetry — the Chaos force had superior individual units but were arranged for march, not defence. The Gitz had inferior units but were arranged for exactly this fight.
- **Design note:** The Anchor-Fall battleplan (Capture-the-Flag/Reality Cores concept) was a designed future battleplan, not this engagement. The actual B6 was a smaller, faster skirmish — tactically decisive but mechanically straightforward.
